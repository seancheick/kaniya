# Kaniya — design context

## Target audience
Pregnant women (first trimester through postpartum, incl. gestational diabetes) and the
partners/friends who buy for them. Later: blood-sugar and heart-health customers. They are
overwhelmed, often nauseous, and anxious about what's safe to eat.

## Use cases
Find comforting, condition-aware snacks without label anxiety; preorder a curated box;
gift a caring box; join waitlists for upcoming boxes; take a 3-question match quiz.

## Brand personality
Warm editorial pantry. Calm, premium, nurturing — never clinical or medical. West
African-rooted name (kɛnɛya = "health" in Dioula). The emotional promise: "someone
thought this through for you."

## Aesthetic system
- Cream `#FAF5EE` paper base, deep cream `#F1E8DC` sections, blush `#EBD8CD` tints,
  sage `#97A68F`/`#5F7057` support, ink `#33302B` text.
- Terracotta `#C2704E` is THE accent: CTAs only, at most one per viewport.
- Fraunces (serif display, italic for emotional emphasis) + Figtree (humanist sans body).
- Left-aligned, asymmetric, generous whitespace; lists and editorial rows over card grids.
- Motion: GSAP only, soft ease `cubic-bezier(0.22,1,0.36,1)`, respect reduced-motion.

## Copy rules (compliance)
"Curated for", "designed with X in mind", "many women reach for" — never "treats",
"relieves", "controls", "safe for all pregnancies". FDA-style disclaimer stays in the
footer. Allergen note: items ship sealed in manufacturer packaging.
